I am a PhD student studying climate change in the <a
href="https://pangea.stanford.edu/researchgroups/cesd/"
target='_blank'>Climate and Earth System Dynamics</a> group at
Stanford. I am interested in the interactions between climate change
and natural weather extremes, particularly extreme precipitation and
flooding. I am particularly excited about applying new quantitative
methods to the field of climate science and hydrology. I also conduct
research to quantify the societal impacts of natural hazards like
flooding. You can read more about my research [here](research).

Prior to graduate school, I worked as a water/civil engineer at
Merrick & Company / McLaughlin Whitewater Design Group in Denver,
CO. I worked on a variety of projects including urban flood risk
reduction, river ecosystem restoration, and whitewater recreation. As
a result, I have technical experience with hydraulic/hydrologic
modeling and floodplain mapping and permitting, and have worked with
local government, non-profit, and federal agencies.

My research-adjacent interests include data visualization and
scientific communication, especially using open-source tools. Some
examples/things I've worked on are [here](other). I also really enjoy
learning new coding/data visualization skills and teaching
computational skills to other scientists (I have taught lessons
through the <a href="https://software-carpentry.org/"
target='_blank'>Software Carpentry organization</a>).

My non-work hobbies include commuting/traveling by bike, floating down a river in my kayak or
raft, and gardening.
